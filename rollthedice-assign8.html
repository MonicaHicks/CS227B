Assignment 7 Writeup

For our Assignment 7 player, we modified our bigSwitch() function to give us better info about the games we were playing. Instead of doing a single depth charge, we run depth charge simulations until we hit the big switch deadline. During these simulations, we keep track of the maximum branching factor, depth, and intermediate ratio we encounter. We track max branching factor by grabbing the maximum number of actions we see throughout the simulated depth charge process. We evaluate max depth by tracking the maximum depth our charge hits. For our intermediate ratio, we just accumulate the intermediate values we see throughout the depth charge and then set the ratio as that value divided by the total nodes we visit on the simulated path.

We also updated our html to display this information on our player page. The primary metric we plan to use when evaluating which player to pick is the intermediate ratio. If a game has very sparse to no intermediate states, we want to use MCS; otherwise, we will use alpha-beta iterative deepening. However, we wanted to track these metrics because we plan to modify the PTS exploration constant for our player based on these values.

For this week’s performance comparisons, here are our results at the following link: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1NqfIHChoxu0TUaeurFJfbzCGxvCUSgR203VxpL0TqOo/edit?usp=sharing

We noticed results similar to what we assessed in class. More specifically, having the grounded rules on the normal interpreter did hurt performance. Including the specialized interpreter for both grounding and symbolizing improved performance for larger games overall, in comparison to non-grounded and non-symbolized games.
